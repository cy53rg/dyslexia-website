Unlocking the superpower of dyslexia

Inspirational speaker

Keisha is a powerful public speaker who inspires audiences with her experiences of Dyslexia and Dyspraxia. She shares her story of inspiration and empowerment locally and globally, providing inspiration to others facing their own challenges. Keisha's coaching skills help individuals overcome a lack of confidence and low self-esteem. Her unique perspective, understanding and empathy have helped countless people to embrace their unique skills and strengths.

Expert Advice and Consultation

Keisha offers Expert Advice and Consultation on dyslexia empowerment, self-advocacy, study skills and more. She provides online and in-person support to help individuals with dyslexia reach their full potential.

Personalised Choaching & Support

Get both one-on-one and group coaching options, as well as online and in-person sessions. A coaching approach that is based on the latest research and best practices in dyslexia education and psychology.

Caro; Stewart

MSc, FlnstLM, Coach, Top LinkedIn Voice, Founder of Northern Black Power Women, Author

Keisha was a speaker at my recent International Women's Day conference and inspired us all with ... her story of courage and determination of growing up severely dyslexic and dyspraxic, but not being aware she had these conditions until the age of 41. Bullied badly as a child, school was a struggle for her and she left school with no GCSEs. In 2016, she gave up her job of 12 years, determined to go to university and study for a degree. It was during the last few weeks of studying for a BSc in Applied Sport and Exercise Science that she was diagnosed with severe dyslexia and dyspraxia. She achieved a First in her degree and even went on to do a masters degree in health psychology and achieved a Merit. This year Keisha was recognised as one of the top 50 Influential Neurodivergent Women 2022. She now speaks on various platforms to increase awareness of the need for neurodiversity, the challenges individuals face, and to empower others with similar experiences as her own. What an amazing women she is. Dyslexic Thinking is now a skill recognised by LinkedIn and they have launched a Dyslexic Thinking skill button that people can add to their profile
